HEJ!

Why do I want to study this course?

I have been interested in Front-end for quite some time now. My journey into programming started with the basics of Python on Codecademy, and after that, I got introduced to HTML and CSS. I fell completely in love with the possibility of creating something with my own head and hands. But very soon I understood that it is really difficult to navigate the whole process without getting feedback from professionals, and it became even more difficult when I got to JavaScript. When I got interested in web design, my mother-in-law wanted to support my passion, so she asked me to create a website for her flower shop. I loved working on this task, though I was a total beginner and it shows (you can check it out here: https://www.knoppochblad.se). I really want to study this course to get better at what I already know and to learn new things that can help me progress in my dream field.

Why do I think that I would be a good future developer?

I think that I would be a good developer because I have two things that I think are vital for programming - focus and passion. I can spend the whole day debugging and trying something new thanks to my ability to focus. And thanks to my passion even when things don't work and I want to give up, something inside me immediately reminds me how much pleasure it brings me to create websites, even if the whole process challenges me. I think that front-end can become my life’s work - and the one thing that I need for it to become true is great teachers. And I am sure that I will find them at Sundsgården Folkhögskola.

A little bit about myself

My full name is Polina, but I prefer Polly (yes, like a parrot or Swedish sweets 😁). Originally I am from Russia, but two years ago I met a Swedish guy while being on covid-lockdown at a hotel in Latvia. We fell in love, and after staying for 6 months in Latvia, we decided to move to Sweden. It was a wonderful change because I adore both Sweden and the Swedish language. Of course, as an expat, I experienced a lot of ups and downs, but no amount of difficulties can take away the feeling that I have finally found a home.

My whole life I was fascinated with books and languages and not so long ago I got a bachelor's degree in literature and a master's degree in linguistics. For some time I worked as a teacher and a translator - and I loved it! But it has always felt that something was missing. So I tried out different jobs and hobbies, but nothing felt right until I came across programming. I was so sure that programming is for those who are great at math and physics, so it was difficult for me to dare to take my new hobby seriously. After a while it got hard to resist the overwhelming interest in programming - I could spend hours sitting in front of the computer, trying to solve a problem in Python or learning how to create different kinds of buttons in HTML and CSS. So I decided that it was time to listen to my inner self and take a chance on making my hobby into my future profession.

Besides programming, I also enjoy reading books, embroidering, doing yoga, drawing, and playing monopoly and cozy video games in my spare time.
